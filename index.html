About


I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Earth Sciences at the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa and also hold an appointment in the International Pacific Research Center. My work combines state-of-the-art statistical techniques and climate models with observed and paleoclimatic data to better understand climate variability and change across timescales. If you are interested in working in my lab as an undergraduate researcher, graduate student or postdoctoral researcher please reach out to discuss opportunities. I was previously at Columbia University for my doctorate and a postdoctoral scientist at the University of Colorado, Boulder and in the Advanced Study Program at the National Center for Atmospheric Research, and an Assistant Scientist in the Department of Geology and Geophysics at the Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution.
